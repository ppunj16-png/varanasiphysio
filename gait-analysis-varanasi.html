Overview

Understand How You Move

Gait analysis is the scientific assessment of how you walk or run. Abnormal movement patterns cause repetitive stress, joint overload, and injury. At VPARC, we use video-based analysis to precisely identify and correct these patterns.

Walking Gait Assessment

Detailed analysis of your walking pattern — step length, cadence, trunk movement, arm swing, and lower limb mechanics.

Running Gait Analysis

Slow-motion video analysis of running mechanics — heel strike, overstriding, cadence, and trunk posture.

Joint-by-Joint Analysis

Measurement of hip, knee, and ankle angles through each phase of the gait cycle.

Corrective Programme

A targeted exercise and retraining programme to correct identified faults and reduce injury risk.

Our Approach

What to Expect at VPARC

1

Video Recording

Walking and/or running on a level surface, recorded from front, side, and rear — slow-motion capability for detailed analysis.

2

Frame-by-Frame Analysis

Your therapist reviews the footage frame by frame, measuring joint angles and identifying deviations from normal patterns.

3

Identification of Key Faults

We identify the 2–3 most significant movement faults contributing to your pain or injury risk.

4

Clinical Report

A written report with video screenshots, measurements, and clinical interpretation.

5

Retraining Programme

A progressive movement retraining programme — specific drills and cues to change your walking or running pattern safely.

Common Questions

FAQs

What should I wear and bring to a gait analysis session?

Wear shorts (above the knee) and your usual exercise shoes or running shoes. If you have orthotics, bring them. For running analysis, wear your typical running kit. The session takes approximately 60–90 minutes.

Can gait analysis help prevent running injuries?

Yes — this is one of its primary uses. Many running injuries (knee pain, shin splints, stress fractures, IT band syndrome) are directly caused by identifiable gait faults. Correcting these through targeted retraining has strong evidence for preventing recurrence.

Is gait analysis useful for children?

Absolutely. Gait analysis is highly valuable for children with toe walking, Cerebral Palsy, in-toeing, out-toeing, and flat feet — helping guide treatment decisions and track improvement over time.
