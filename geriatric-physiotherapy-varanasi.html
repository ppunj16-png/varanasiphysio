Overview

Dignified, Independent Ageing

Geriatric physiotherapy addresses the unique needs of older adults — age-related changes in strength, balance, flexibility, and bone density. At VPARC, we help elderly patients stay active, safe, and independent.

Fall Prevention Programmes

Structured balance and strength training to significantly reduce fall risk — the leading cause of injury in the elderly.

Arthritis Management

Evidence-based physiotherapy for osteoarthritis and rheumatoid arthritis — reducing pain and improving joint function without medication dependency.

Functional Independence

Training in daily tasks: rising from chairs, climbing stairs, walking outdoors — the activities that define independence.

Home Safety Assessment

Evaluation of your home environment and practical recommendations to reduce fall hazards.

Common Questions

FAQs

Is physiotherapy safe for elderly patients with osteoporosis?

Yes, when properly assessed and tailored. Physiotherapy for osteoporosis focuses on safe loading exercises that stimulate bone density, balance training to prevent falls, and posture work. We avoid high-impact activities and always review bone density reports before starting.

Can physiotherapy help an elderly person who has become very weak and bed-bound?

Absolutely. We have extensive experience working with deconditioned, bed-bound, or post-hospitalisation elderly patients. Treatment starts very gently — in bed if needed — and progressively builds towards sitting, standing, and walking. Home visits make this especially practical.

How often should elderly patients attend physiotherapy?

For acute conditions or post-surgical rehab, 3–5 sessions per week initially. For maintenance, strength, and balance programmes, 2–3 sessions per week is typical. Many elderly patients benefit from an ongoing monthly maintenance programme to preserve function long-term.
