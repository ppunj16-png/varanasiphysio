Overview

Specialist Care for Women's Health

Women's health physiotherapy addresses conditions unique to or more common in women — from pregnancy-related pain to pelvic floor dysfunction. VPARC provides this care in a private, sensitive environment by experienced female-friendly therapists.

Pelvic Floor Rehabilitation

Assessment and treatment of pelvic floor muscle dysfunction — the foundation of bladder, bowel, and pelvic organ health.

Prenatal Physiotherapy

Managing pregnancy-related back pain, pelvic girdle pain, and preparing the body for labour and delivery.

Postnatal Rehabilitation

Safe, progressive return to activity after childbirth — addressing diastasis recti, pelvic floor recovery, and posture.

Private Environment

All women's health sessions are conducted in complete privacy. Same-gender therapist available on request.

Common Questions

FAQs

Is it normal to have leakage after childbirth?

Urinary incontinence after childbirth is very common — but it is NOT normal or something to simply accept. Pelvic floor physiotherapy has excellent evidence for resolving post-natal incontinence. Most women see significant improvement within 6–8 weeks of starting treatment.

When can I start physiotherapy after a caesarean section?

Gentle pelvic floor exercises can begin within days of a C-section. Physiotherapy for scar tissue, core rehabilitation, and progressive return to activity typically starts at 6 weeks post-surgery with medical clearance. VPARC offers home visits for new mothers in the early weeks.

Can physiotherapy treat pelvic organ prolapse without surgery?

Yes, in mild-to-moderate prolapse, pelvic floor physiotherapy is the first-line treatment and achieves excellent results — often avoiding or significantly delaying surgery. It involves pelvic floor muscle training, lifestyle advice, and load management strategies.
