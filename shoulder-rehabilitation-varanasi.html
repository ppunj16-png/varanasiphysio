Overview

Varanasi's Shoulder Rehabilitation Specialists

The shoulder is the most complex and mobile joint in the body — and the most commonly injured. At VPARC, we have developed deep expertise in all shoulder conditions, combining advanced manual therapy, targeted strengthening, and scapular control retraining for complete functional recovery.

Rotator Cuff Expertise

Deep specialist knowledge of all four rotator cuff muscles, their roles, injury patterns, and rehabilitation protocols.

Scapular Control Retraining

The scapula (shoulder blade) is the foundation of all shoulder movement — we systematically retrain its control.

Post-Surgical Protocols

Phase-based rehabilitation after rotator cuff repair, SLAP repair, shoulder replacement, and stabilisation surgery.

Movement Analysis

Video-based movement analysis to identify and correct faulty shoulder mechanics that cause pain or re-injury.

Common Questions

FAQs

How long does frozen shoulder take to recover?

Frozen shoulder typically resolves in 12–24 months without treatment. With physiotherapy, the process can be accelerated significantly — most patients see major improvement within 3–4 months of consistent treatment. VPARC uses aggressive stretching, manual therapy, and mobilisation techniques.

Do I need surgery for a rotator cuff tear?

Not necessarily. Research shows that physiotherapy achieves outcomes comparable to surgery for most partial tears and even many full-thickness tears in non-athletic patients. VPARC will assess your tear and give you an honest recommendation. We coordinate with surgeons when surgery is genuinely indicated.

How long after rotator cuff surgery should I wait before physiotherapy?

Physiotherapy begins immediately after surgery — gentle pendulum exercises start day 1. Passive range of motion begins at 1–2 weeks. Active strengthening typically starts at 6–8 weeks depending on the size of the repair and surgeon's protocol. VPARC works within your surgeon's specific guidelines.
