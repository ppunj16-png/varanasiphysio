Common Questions

FAQs

When should I start physiotherapy after knee replacement?

Physiotherapy typically starts the day after surgery in hospital and continues as outpatient rehabilitation from day 3–5. Early mobility is critical for preventing stiffness and blood clots. At VPARC, we can visit you at home in the first week if needed.

What is prehabilitation and do I need it?

Prehabilitation is physiotherapy done before your surgery to strengthen muscles and improve mobility. Research consistently shows that patients who prehab have faster recovery, shorter hospital stays, and better functional outcomes. VPARC recommends at least 4–6 weeks of prehab before elective surgery.

How long does full recovery take after total knee replacement?

Most patients are walking independently by 6 weeks and return to normal daily activities by 3 months. Full recovery — including stair climbing, kneeling, and recreational activities — typically takes 9–12 months with proper rehabilitation.
