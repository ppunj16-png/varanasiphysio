Common Questions

FAQs

Is tele-physiotherapy as effective as in-person sessions?

For exercise-based rehabilitation, follow-up care, and chronic pain management, tele-physiotherapy has been shown to be equally effective as in-person care in multiple studies. It is not suitable for conditions requiring hands-on manual therapy or electrotherapy — for those, in-clinic sessions are recommended.

What equipment do I need for a tele-physiotherapy session?

You need a smartphone, tablet, or laptop with a camera and internet connection. A clear space to move around (about 2 metres) and comfortable clothing. No special equipment is needed for most assessment sessions — your therapist will advise on any basic tools needed for your exercise programme.

Can I get a prescription or medical certificate via tele-physiotherapy?

Physiotherapists can provide an exercise prescription, home programme documentation, and a clinical report via tele-consultation. For formal medical certificates or referrals, an in-person assessment at VPARC may be required.
